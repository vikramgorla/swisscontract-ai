─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. POSITION AND DUTIES

1.1  The Employee is hired in the role of Senior Software Engineer,
     reporting to the Head of Engineering.

1.2  The Employee shall perform all duties assigned by the Company in
     good faith and in accordance with Art. 321a OR. The Company
     reserves the right to assign the Employee to other tasks of
     comparable responsibility with reasonable notice.

1.3  The place of work is Zürich. The Company may require the Employee
     to work from other locations or travel on Company business as the
     role demands.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2. COMMENCEMENT AND PROBATIONARY PERIOD

2.1  The employment relationship commences on 1 April 2026.

2.2  The first three (3) months constitute a probationary period
     (Art. 335b OR). During the probationary period, either party may
     terminate this contract at any time with seven (7) days' written
     notice.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3. WORKING HOURS

3.1  The standard working week is forty-two (42) hours, distributed
     over five days Monday to Friday.

3.2  The Employee may be required to work additional hours in accordance
     with Art. 321c OR. Overtime shall be compensated by time off in
     lieu or, at the Company's discretion, by additional remuneration
     at the ordinary hourly rate. No overtime premium is paid.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4. REMUNERATION

4.1  The Employee shall receive a gross annual salary of CHF 95,000
     (ninety-five thousand Swiss francs), paid in twelve equal monthly
     instalments of CHF 7,916.67 on the last working day of each month.

4.2  Social security contributions (AHV/IV/EO, ALV, SUVA/accident
     insurance) shall be deducted in accordance with applicable Swiss
     law.

4.3  Any salary adjustment is at the sole discretion of the Company and
     does not create a precedent or entitlement for future increases.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5. ANNUAL VACATION

5.1  The Employee is entitled to twenty (20) working days of paid
     vacation per calendar year (Art. 329a OR), pro-rated in the first
     and last year of employment.

5.2  Vacation dates must be agreed with the line manager in advance.
     The Company may, in exceptional business circumstances, postpone
     previously approved vacation with reasonable notice.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6. NOTICE PERIODS

6.1  During the probationary period (clause 2.2), notice of seven (7)
     days applies (Art. 335b OR).

6.2  After completion of the probationary period, the notice period is
     three (3) months to the end of a calendar month (Art. 335c OR).

6.3  Notice must be given in writing. Termination during periods of
     protected absence (illness, accident, pregnancy) is governed by
     Art. 336c OR.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

7. CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1  The Employee shall keep strictly confidential, both during and
     after the employment relationship, all trade secrets, technical
     know-how, business strategies, client lists, pricing, financial
     information, and any other non-public information of the Company
     or its clients (Art. 321a para. 4 OR).

7.2  Upon termination of employment for any reason, the Employee shall
     immediately return all documents, data carriers, and materials
     belonging to the Company, and shall permanently delete any copies
     retained on personal devices.

7.3  The confidentiality obligation survives termination of this
     contract without time limit.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

8. NON-COMPETE CLAUSE

8.1  For a period of twelve (12) months following termination of this
     employment contract — for any reason — the Employee shall not,
     directly or indirectly:

     (a) engage in, or hold any ownership interest in, any business
         that competes with the Company's business activities;

     (b) solicit or accept business from any client or prospect of the
         Company with whom the Employee had material contact during the
         last twenty-four (24) months of employment;

     (c) solicit, recruit, or employ any current employee or contractor
         of the Company.

8.2  The geographic scope of this restriction is the entire territory
     of Switzerland.

8.3  No financial compensation is payable by the Company in respect of
     the non-compete obligation.

8.4  The Company reserves the right to seek injunctive relief and
     damages in the event of breach, in accordance with Art. 340b OR.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

9.1  All work results, inventions, software, and other intellectual
     property created by the Employee in the course of employment — or
     using Company resources — vest exclusively in the Company upon
     creation (Art. 332 OR).

9.2  The Employee assigns to the Company all rights not automatically
     transferred by law, and waives any moral rights to the extent
     permitted by Swiss law.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

10. APPLICABLE LAW AND JURISDICTION

10.1 This contract is governed by Swiss law, in particular the Swiss
     Code of Obligations (Art. 319–362 OR).

10.2 The exclusive place of jurisdiction for any disputes arising from
     or in connection with this contract is Zürich, Switzerland,
     subject to mandatory statutory provisions (Art. 34 ZPO).

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

11. MISCELLANEOUS

11.1 Should any provision of this contract be or become invalid or
     unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force.
     The invalid provision shall be replaced by a valid provision that
     most closely reflects the original intent.

11.2 Amendments to this contract must be made in writing and signed by
     both parties.

11.3 This contract supersedes all prior oral or written agreements
     between the parties relating to the subject matter hereof.
